Understanding CSS Cascade Layers

Cascade layers give you explicit control over the order of precedence in your stylesheet. No more fighting specificity — just declare your layers and let the cascade do its job.

By Jane Smith · May 18, 2026 · 12 min read

What are cascade layers?

Introduced in CSS 2022, @layer lets you group rules into named layers and declare their priority order upfront. A rule in a later layer always beats a rule in an earlier layer, regardless of specificity. 

@layer reset, base, components, utilities;

That one declaration fixes the entire precedence hierarchy for your stylesheet. Utilities will always override components; components will always override base styles.

Why specificity stops mattering

Without layers, a low-specificity utility rule can be defeated by a high-specificity component rule written earlier in the file. With layers, the layer order is the final word — specificity only breaks ties within the same layer.

This means you can write utilities as single-class selectors and trust they will win over multi-class component selectors, as long as utilities is declared last.

Layers in Drizzle CSS

Drizzle declares eleven layers in a fixed order: 

@layer tokens, dark, reset, base, typography, lists, tables, forms, media, utilities, palette;

palette sits last so colour modifiers like .bg-red always win over layout utilities — even when both set background. This was a real bug in the first build; the fix was a single import-order swap.
